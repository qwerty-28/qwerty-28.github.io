Spam musubi is a famous Hawaiian snack made with Spam, rice and seaweed. It is portable, affordable and tasty. It is the most popular way to eat Spam in Hawaii.

What is Spam musubi?

Spam musubi is a local Hawaiian snack. It is a small portable snack made with three main ingredients: spam, rice and seaweed. The ham is placed on a bed of rice and the whole thing is wrapped in seaweed. They are affordable, convenient and very tasty. In Hawaii, Spam musubi can be found everywhere, including 7-Eleven, supermarket delis and family food places. It can also be made at home.

Where does Spam musubi come from?

Spam became popular in Hawaii just after World War II. During the war, the military consumed large amounts of Spam. Since many troops were stationed in Hawaii, there was a lot of Spam. People liked the taste and eventually "adopted" Spam in Hawaii. Ham musubi was invented by a real Japanese woman in Hawaii.

Why is ham musubi so popular in Hawaii?

Out.

Hawaii is very open and Spam musubi is the ultimate portable snack. You can pack it in your backpack before you go hiking or take it to the beach (put a few musubi in your beach bag and snack on it when you get out of the water). Ham musubi don't need to be refrigerated and last a long time, so they're great after a day of physical activity outdoors. Just make sure to bring water or a drink.

Families/groups

Hawaii values families and groups. It is not uncommon to find several generations living together in one house. Spam Musubi is a great choice for family reunions, garage parties, bake sales (with butter mocha!), and especially at group events like after-school football games. They're easy to eat and everyone loves them. It's not hard, it's not unnecessary, it's just delicious.
